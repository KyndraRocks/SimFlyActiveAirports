?  How to Use This Tool
✈
Point-to-Point Distance
Type any two ICAO codes into the Departure and Arrival fields to instantly calculate the great-circle distance in nautical miles. The result appears to the right of the inputs. Fields turn red if an ICAO isn't found in the database. Press Enter or Tab to move between fields; Escape clears both.
🗺️
File on SimBrief
Once both departure and arrival ICAOs are valid, the File on SimBrief → button lights up. Click it to open SimBrief's dispatch page pre-filled with your route — ready to generate a full flight plan in one click.

Hover the button to preview exactly what will be sent: origin, destination, aircraft type (if one is selected), and flight number. The flight number is synthesized from the 3-letter owner codes of the departure and arrival airport owners.
✈️
Aircraft Panel
Click the Aircraft header to expand the panel. Use the category tabs to filter by aircraft type, then click any chip to select an aircraft. The spec strip shows takeoff distance, landing distance, and range — both at full MTOW and adjusted for your current fuel and payload settings.

The Fuel slider sets fuel load as a percentage of max capacity. The Payload slider sets passenger/cargo load as a percentage of max payload. Both sliders feed into the adjusted performance column and affect the suitability check. The stacked Weights bar shows OEW, payload, and fuel as proportions of MTOW.

When an aircraft is selected, the Filter airports unsuitable for aircraft button appears below the matrix. Click it to dim any airport the selected aircraft cannot use — based on runway length, surface type (paved vs. unpaved), SimFly category limits, and whether the destination is within range from the current departure. The filter re-evaluates automatically whenever you change the departure airport or adjust the fuel or payload sliders.

Density-altitude correction — required runway distance is automatically scaled for density altitude using a per-category factor (light pistons feel DA the most, jets the least). When you've fetched weather, the airport's METAR temperature and altimeter setting drive the calculation; otherwise the app falls back to ISA conditions from the field elevation alone. The suitability tooltip shows the DA used and its source — e.g. DA 11,400 ft (METAR, 14:30Z) or DA 9,228 ft (ISA — fetch weather for accuracy). Cold or low-pressure airports never reduce required distance below book numbers.
ℹ️
Airport Info & External Links
Hover over any ICAO label — in the matrix, or in the airport name lines below the inputs — to see a popup with the airport's full name, IATA code, city, country, elevation, and runway details (lengths, surfaces, and lighting).

The airport name lines are also clickable links to the SimFly airport details page. Small icon buttons beside each name open Flightsim.to and FS Addon Compare search results for that ICAO in a new tab. Tip — hold Shift to open these in the background.
⛅
Weather & Flight Conditions
Click the ⛅ button in the top-left corner of the distance matrix to fetch live METAR data for every airport currently in view. Each ICAO label gets a colored dot showing its flight category: VFR (green), MVFR (blue), IFR (red), or LIFR (magenta). Hover any dot to see the full decoded report — conditions, wind, visibility, ceiling, observation time, and raw METAR text.

If an airport has no reporting station of its own, the nearest one within 100 nm is used as a fallback, shown with a dashed ring around the dot. Weather data is cached for 30 minutes; switching owner dropdowns clears the cache and prompts a fresh fetch. If the Distance Map panel is open when you fetch, map marker rings update to show flight categories too. Distance Map info cards below the map also include a Weather section with the same decoded report whenever METAR data is cached for the highlighted route's airports.
📋
Owner Distance Matrix
Select a Departure Airport Owner and an Arrival Airport Owner from the dropdowns to build a full distance matrix — every departure airport on the rows, every arrival airport on the columns. The brightest yellow cell marks the longest route currently visible within the active distance filter range.

Click any distance cell to highlight its row and column and populate the Departure and Arrival fields above with those ICAOs.

The ⇄ button between the owner dropdowns swaps the Departure and Arrival selections instantly.
↕️
Row & Column Sorting
The Dep and Arr sort pills below the matrix control the ordering of departure rows and arrival columns independently. Click either pill to cycle through: A–Z → Nearest → Farthest → A–Z. A brighter pill indicates an active (non-alphabetical) sort.

Click a row label (departure ICAO) to highlight that row. A second click on the same label sorts arrival columns using whichever mode the Arr pill currently shows — then the pill advances to preview the next step. A third click applies the new mode, and so on. Clicking a different row label moves the highlight without changing the sort.

Click a column header (arrival ICAO) to highlight that column. A second click on the same header sorts departure rows using whichever mode the Dep pill currently shows — then the pill advances. Clicking a different column header moves the highlight without changing the sort.

Sticky sort — click the padlock icon on the right side of either pill to lock it. When locked, the pill turns orange and the first click on any row or column label sorts immediately without needing a second click. Click the padlock again to unlock.

Tip — hovering any row label or column header shows a tooltip indicating what the next click will do.
🎚️
Distance Filter
The dual-handle slider below the matrix filters routes by distance. Drag the left handle to set a minimum and the right handle to set a maximum. Routes outside the range are dimmed; routes within range remain fully visible. The longest route within the active range is highlighted in yellow. You can also drag the filled bar itself to pan the range window without changing its width.
🏷️
Category Filter
The numbered circle buttons (1–7) below the sort pills filter the matrix by SimFly airport category. All categories are shown by default. Click a circle to toggle that category off or on — airports in hidden categories are dimmed in both their ICAO labels and their distance cells. All categories can be toggled off simultaneously.

Invert flips the current selection — active categories become inactive and vice versa, including toggling all off when all are on.

Enable Hide filtered (below the circles) to remove airports from the matrix entirely rather than dimming them. This applies to airports in hidden categories, and also to any row or column where every route falls outside the active distance filter range. Row and column highlights on hidden airports are cleared automatically.
🌍
Route Map
Click the ROUTE MAP header below the matrix to expand the interactive map panel. Every departure and arrival airport appears as a pin — departure pins use a dark fill, arrival pins use a lighter fill. Clicking any pin zooms and pans the map to show the full set of visible airports at once.

When a distance cell is active (row and column highlighted), an airport info strip appears below the map with the full details for both airports. If weather has been fetched, each pin's ring shows its flight category color — a legend in the map corner explains the scheme.

Use the 🛰 Satellite button in the top-right corner of the map to toggle between satellite imagery and street map view.
⊙
Region Mode
Click ⊙ Regions in the panel header to open the Region Mode map. All SimFly player-owned airports are shown as color-coded dots (matching each airport’s SimFly category color). Hover any dot to see a tooltip with the ICAO, category, airport name, owner, and longest runway details after a brief delay.

Step 1 — Departure region: click and hold anywhere on the map to place the departure center, then drag outward to draw a circle. The radius line and live nm distance guide your selection. Release to lock the region. If an aircraft is selected in the main UI, a dashed gold ring shows its effective range from that center.

Step 2 — Arrival region: after releasing, the cursor switches to ruler mode — moving the mouse shows the distance from the departure center. Click and drag again to draw the arrival region circle the same way.

Once both regions are set, a results bar appears with controls arranged around the centered route count. On the left: Sort order, a Dep Owners ▾ dropdown, and the Dep category filter group. On the right: the Arr category filter group, an Arr Owners ▾ dropdown, and the Max per region cap. The two owner menus filter independently — use Dep Owners to restrict departure rows and Arr Owners to restrict arrival columns. Each menu has a search box at the top: type any letters to instantly narrow the list to matching owners; press Enter to clear the search and return to the full list (selections are preserved). While search text is active, All and None apply only to the visible rows — hidden rows keep their current state. Use All or None with no search text to select or clear the complete list, or toggle individual owners by clicking their row. The matrix and map dots update in real time as you filter. If at least one owner is hidden, moving or resizing a region preserves that selection — hidden owners stay hidden, and any new owners entering the region appear visible by default. If all owners are visible, the list auto-refreshes when the region changes.

Map dot selection — click any airport dot on the map to build a route using a rolling chain: the first click sets the departure; the second click sets the arrival and draws the route. Each subsequent click advances the chain — the previous arrival becomes the new departure and the clicked airport becomes the new arrival. This works on both the colored in-region dots and the background dots outside the region circles. Selecting a departure/arrival pair this way immediately updates the main screen departure and arrival fields and triggers owner auto-selection — no separate Send to Flight Planner click needed for that. Double-click any dot to zoom the map to that airport instead; double-clicking does not advance the routing chain.

Map category filter — the Map filter buttons at the top of the region screen also control the main screen departure and arrival category filters: toggling a category on the map instantly applies the same selection to both the Dep and Arr filter rows on the main screen.

Airport owner filter — the Airport Owners ▾ dropdown in the header bar filters which owners’ airports appear as dots on the map. It operates independently of the Dep Owners and Arr Owners controls (which affect only the matrix rows and columns) — use Airport Owners to declutter the map to just the pilots you care about while leaving the matrix unaffected. The panel behaves identically to the dep/arr owner menus: type to search, press Enter to clear the search, use All / None to bulk-select, arrow keys to navigate, and Space to toggle a highlighted row.

Sticky headers — departure row labels stay pinned to the left edge and arrival column headers stay pinned to the top as you scroll a large matrix, keeping airport identifiers visible at all times.

Owner stripe — each row and column header shows a thin colored ribbon for the airport owner. Click the ribbon to instantly filter the matrix to that owner only; a second click restores the full list.

Drag the thin handle between the map and the results bar to resize the map vs. matrix area. Click any matrix cell to select a route; the Send to Flight Planner → button closes the modal and populates the main fields.

Airport search — a search box floats in the top-left corner of the map. Type an ICAO code, airport name, or city to filter all loaded airports; up to 10 matches appear ranked by exactness. Click a result or press ↑/↓ to navigate and Enter to select — the map flies to that airport. Press Esc or click ✕ to dismiss.

Use ↩ Undo to step back one phase (removes the last region drawn). Click ⊙ DONE (results phase only) to collapse the distance matrix and return to free map exploration — region circles stay on the map so you can still reference them. Click ⊙ Select Regions to start a fresh selection (this clears any previous circles). Use ✕ Close to exit Region Mode entirely. The main-screen ✕ Reset button also closes Region Mode and clears all region state.
